# Studio Twenty Six

Use this brief to generate high-fidelity website screens for `Studio Twenty Six`.

## Direction Shift

Move away from brutalist energy.

The site should feel:
- vintage
- handmade
- collage-inspired
- warm and artistic
- confident but not harsh
- community-first
- feminine without becoming precious

Avoid:
- brutalist heaviness
- aggressive black borders everywhere
- overly rigid grid systems
- stark modern minimalism
- tech/startup UI patterns

The reference feeling is:
- bold editorial hierarchy
- GRLSWIRL-style confidence and community presence
- handmade imperfection
- art-poster softness
- tactile collage composition

## Core Aesthetic

The visual language should combine:
- oversized typography
- organic shapes
- subtle rotations
- layered imagery
- soft vintage color
- blurred background forms
- generous whitespace
- tactile framing

This should feel like:
- a community art collective
- a vintage editorial brand
- a collage-based creative studio
- an artist-made site, not a SaaS site

## Color Palette

### Primary Structure
- Deep Teal: `#2B4A4C`
- Charcoal: `#3A3E42`

### Light Foundations
- Off White / Canvas: `#F5F3ED`
- Warm Beige: `#E5D5BF`

### Accents
- Burnt Coral: `#D97854`
- Dusty Blue: `#9FB8BE`
- Sage Green: `#8B9E7E`
- Salmon Pink: `#E89C8F`
- Peachy Cream: `#E8C5A8`
- Olive Green: `#6B7252`

## Palette Intent

This palette should create:
- vintage handmade atmosphere
- artistic softness
- earthy warmth
- readable contrast
- a painterly, collected feeling

Use:
- `#F5F3ED` as the primary canvas background
- `#2B4A4C` for strong structure and major text moments
- `#D97854` and `#E89C8F` for warmth and CTAs
- `#9FB8BE` and `#8B9E7E` for softer supporting sections

## Typography

Use:
- Headline: bold oversized display type
- Body: clean, legible editorial sans or serif-sans hybrid
- Labels / utility text: mono or small uppercase supporting type

Target feeling:
- bold and confident
- soft-edged rather than severe
- editorial and artistic

Typography should feel:
- expressive in headlines
- spacious in body copy
- readable and welcoming

## Layout System

The layout should feel bold but breathable.

Use:
- generous whitespace
- asymmetry
- collage-style composition
- image overlap
- soft section transitions
- rotated frames by `-1deg` to `1deg`

Do not use:
- rigid brutalist blocks
- heavy black outlines everywhere
- dense card walls
- dashboard rhythm

## Shapes and Surfaces

Prefer:
- soft organic background blobs
- rounded or slightly softened containers
- layered paper-like panels
- framed images that feel like physical prints
- subtle texture and warmth

Image frames can be:
- thick bordered like mounted prints
- slightly rotated
- offset from surrounding content

## Motion

Use smooth, elegant transitions throughout.

Recommended:
- hover scale on images
- hover lift on buttons
- subtle fade/slide transitions
- scene transitions that feel fluid and editorial

Do not make motion feel:
- mechanical
- overly bouncy
- app-like

## Navigation

Navigation should feel elevated and editorial.

Include:
- sticky nav
- search icon
- account icon
- cart icon

Even if the current site does not need commerce yet, the screen language can borrow that richer community/lifestyle brand structure.

## Components

### Buttons
- bold and clear
- slightly soft corners or clean rounded rectangles
- hover shift or lift
- use coral / teal / sage strategically

### Cards
- rotated slightly for handmade feel
- soft gradients allowed
- layered or overlapping arrangements encouraged
- avoid identical card repetition

### Inputs
- rounded or softly framed
- clean and welcoming
- should feel editorial, not enterprise form UI

### Background Elements
- blurred organic shapes
- soft collage layering
- tonal depth rather than harsh borders

## Image Direction

Use imagery that emphasizes:
- art in process
- tactile materials
- layered collage feeling
- painted surfaces
- paper, textiles, clay, costume, and handwork
- women-led creative community
- warmth and human presence

The photography should feel:
- tactile
- warm
- slightly vintage
- not sterile
- not generic corporate stock

### Key Message
`Messy hands. Hot colors. Real community.`

### Home Sections

1. Sticky Navigation
- logo
- links
- search icon
- account icon
- cart icon

2. Hero Section
- oversized headline
- strong community-first message
- two CTAs
- large framed images

3. Featured Collection / Featured Studio Focus
- use the Art Nouveau reference image as inspiration for this section’s mood
- large featured image in a rotated frame
- hover effect on image

4. Community / Chapters Style Grid
- reinterpret this for Studio Twenty Six programs or creative circles
- gradient cards
- soft rotation

- showcase the collage art energy

- rounded input
- easy CTA

7. Footer
- organized links

## Programs Page

### Goal
Make programs feel like a stylish editorial schedule, not a utility calendar.

### Sections
- intro hero
- featured monthly rhythm
- recurring classes shown as editorial blocks
- highlighted sessions with art-led imagery
- pricing shown simply and beautifully

Use:
- collage stacking
- rotated session cards
- color-blocked rows
- clear hierarchy

## About Page

### Goal
Tell the studio story in a way that feels warm, creative, and culture-led.

### Sections
- editorial hero
- mission statement
- studio ethos
- communal values
- partnership invitation

The about page should feel intimate and artful, not institutional.

## Contact Page

### Goal
Make contact feel stylish, easy, and human.

### Sections
- intro hero
- contact details
- form
- access note

The form should feel:
- calm
- well spaced
- softly framed
- warm rather than bureaucratic

## Register Page

### Goal
Make registration feel simple and welcoming.

### Sections
- hero
- single-session signup
- side note about sliding scale
- supporting details

Important copy to preserve:
`If you can't pay the listed price, say that in the notes. We will follow up with a sliding scale option.`

## The Bus Page

### Goal
Present the bus as a mobile extension of the same artistic world.

### Sections
- hero
- what the bus does
- who it serves
- visual storytelling
- inquiry CTA

The bus page should feel more like a traveling art installation than a municipal program.

## Tone of Voice

Voice should be:
- confident
- artistic
- warm
- clear
- community-centered
- slightly playful

Avoid:
- tech language
- therapy clichés
- hard-edged manifesto language
- corporate sales tone

## Screen Generation Notes

When generating screens:
- lean into bold type
- preserve lots of whitespace
- use asymmetry
- add subtle rotations
- include soft gradients
- use tactile framed imagery
- keep the handmade energy visible

Do not generate:
- brutalist black-box layouts
- startup landing page patterns
- generic rounded wellness brand cliches
- plain utility forms without personality
